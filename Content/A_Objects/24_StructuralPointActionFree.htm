Free point load

The Free point load is related to slabs. The load is not defined by the entity it acts on, but by a specific load point. Free loads are defined by means of "loading entities" that may overlap or affect one or more slabs.

The validity of free load means which particular slabs are subject to the given load and the halfspace where the load acts.

All - Both entities located under and above the defined load are subject to the defined load.

MInusZ (-Z) - The load is supposed to act only in the space located along the negative half of Z-axis of the LCS.

PLusZ (+Z) - The load is supposed to act only in the space located along the positive half of Z-axis of the LCS.

FromTo - The load acts in these borders of Z-axis

ZIsZero (Z = 0) - Only the entities located in the XY-plane of the LCS are subject to the load in question.

ZMInusZero (-Z+0) - The load is supposed to act only in the space located along the negative half of Z-axis of the LCS and in the XY-plane.

ZPlusZero (+Z+0) - The load is supposed to act only in the space located along the positive half of Z-axis of the LCS and in the XY-plane.
Validity from [m] Double 2 Validity from is only used when Validity is set to From-to
Validity to [m] Double 5 Validity to is only used when Validity is set to From-to
Value [kN] Double -1 Specifies the size of the load
Load case String LC2 The name of the load case to which the force belongs
Coordinate X [m] Double -1,000 The X coordinate of the point force
Coordinate Y [m] Double 1,000 The Y coordinate of the point force
Coordinate Z [m] Double 3,250 The Z coordinate of the point force
Coordinate system String Global

Defines the co-ordinate system in which the load is applied. Only global coordinate system is supported for free point loads
